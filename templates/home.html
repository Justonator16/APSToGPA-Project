What is APS and GPA?

APS

APS stands for "Admission Point Score" in South Africa. The Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET) in South Africa uses a method called the Admission Point Score to determine applicants' eligibility for admission to public universities .

Based on a student's final matriculation (Grade 12) exam results, the APS is determined. The student's APS is the total of the points earned in several courses, and each matric subject is given a particular number of points. Depending on the university, the course of study, and the degree of entrance competition, different APS criteria apply.

The table below shows the typical APS Scrore points corresponding to different grades:

Grade Range APS Level
80%-100% 7
70%-79% 6
60%-69% 5
50%-59% 4
40%-49% 3
30%-49% 2
0%-29% 1

GPA

The term GPA refers to the "Grade Point Average". The student's GPA is the sum of points earned across various courses, where each matric subject corresponds to a specific number of points. Different GPA requirements apply based on the university, the chosen field of study, and the level of competition for admission.

You can determine your letter grade and grade points based on the final grade of a specific subject or GPA for a group of subjects. The subsequent table illustrates the usual GPA grade assignments corresponding to different percentage ranges:

Grade Range GPA Grade
80%-100% A
70%-79% B
60%-69% C
50%-59% D
40%-49% E
30%-0% F
